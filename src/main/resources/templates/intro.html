Crypto Terminology Explained

Starting your cryptocurrency journey? Learn the essential terms and metrics used in crypto analysis.

OHLCV Explained

Open

The price of an asset at the beginning of a specific time period (e.g. the start of the day).

High

The highest price reached during that time period.

Low

The lowest price reached during that time period.

Close

The price at the end of the time period. This is often the most important value for analysis.

Volume

The total amount of the asset traded during the time period. High volume usually indicates strong market interest.

OHLCV data is the foundation of candlestick charts and technical analysis.

On-Chain Metrics

Active Addresses

The number of unique wallet addresses that were active on the blockchain. A higher number suggests increased network usage.

Transactions

The total number of transactions processed on the blockchain in a given period.

MVRV Ratio

Market Value to Realized Value ratio. It helps identify whether an asset is overvalued or undervalued.

Network Hash Rate

The total computational power securing the blockchain. Higher hash rate generally means higher network security.

Exchange Inflow

The amount of cryptocurrency sent to exchanges. High inflow may indicate selling pressure.

Exchange Outflow

The amount of cryptocurrency withdrawn from exchanges. Often associated with long-term holding.

Net Flow

The difference between inflow and outflow. Negative net flow suggests accumulation.

Technical Analysis Indicators

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

Measures momentum on a scale from 0 to 100. Values above 70 indicate overbought conditions, below 30 oversold.

MACD

Moving Average Convergence Divergence. Shows trend direction and momentum using moving averages.

Stochastic Oscillator

Compares closing price to a price range over time to identify overbought and oversold conditions.

ADX

Average Directional Index. Measures the strength of a trend, regardless of direction.

CCI

Commodity Channel Index. Identifies cyclical trends and extreme price movements.

SMA / EMA / WMA

Different types of moving averages. SMA is simple, EMA reacts faster to price changes, WMA prioritizes recent prices.

Bollinger Bands

Volatility bands placed above and below a moving average. Used to identify price extremes.

Volume SMA

A moving average of trading volume used to confirm trend strength.
